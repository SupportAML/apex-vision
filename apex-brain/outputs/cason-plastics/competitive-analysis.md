## 1. Market Overview

### Market Size and Growth

The U.S. aesthetic surgery procedures market was valued at approximately $18.24 billion in 2024 and is projected to grow at a CAGR of 11.6% through 2030. Dallas-Fort Worth is one of the top-tracked metropolitan markets nationally, identified by major research firms as a significant and growing hub for plastic surgery.

### DFW Market Characteristics

- **High saturation, moderate fragmentation.** The DFW metroplex has one of the highest concentrations of board-certified plastic surgeons in the South. The market is highly fragmented -- no single practice holds more than 5% market share nationally, and the same applies locally. This means there is room for well-positioned newcomers, but differentiation is essential.
- **UT Southwestern pipeline dominance.** A disproportionate number of DFW plastic surgeons trained at or are affiliated with UT Southwestern Medical Center. This creates a network effect that benefits those surgeons but also means the market is saturated with a similar training pedigree -- making alternative training backgrounds (Duke, Stanford, Harvard) a genuine differentiator.
- **Natural aesthetics philosophy.** The DFW market skews toward natural-looking results. Patients here value subtle enhancement over dramatic transformation -- a philosophy that aligns with Dr. Cason's approach.
- **Price advantage vs. coasts.** Procedures in Dallas cost 30-50% less than identical services in Beverly Hills or Manhattan, making the region attractive for medical tourism and price-conscious patients seeking top-tier surgeons.
- **GLP-1 tailwind.** The 300% surge in GLP-1 weight-loss drug prescriptions (Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro) is generating significant incremental demand for body contouring, skin tightening, and post-weight-loss procedures -- a growing segment that savvy practices are already targeting.
- **Consolidation pressure.** Multi-specialty clinic chains are consolidating, raising competitive thresholds in marketing and digital engagement. Independent practices must invest in digital presence to remain competitive.

## 3. Cason's Competitive Advantages

### 3.1 Duke Training (vs. the UTSW Default)

Dr. Cason completed a full **6-year integrated plastic surgery residency at Duke** -- one of the most prestigious programs in the country. Duke's program is known for its comprehensive exposure to both reconstructive and aesthetic surgery, microsurgery, and craniofacial work.

**Only Dr. Khan shares a Duke connection**, and hers is medical school only (residency was at University of South Florida). Dr. Cason is the only DFW surgeon who completed his full plastic surgery residency at Duke.

This dual expertise means:

## 6. Actionable Recommendations

### Website -- Immediate Priorities

1. **Create dedicated procedure pages with location modifiers.** Every key procedure (breast augmentation, mommy makeover, facelift, rhinoplasty, tummy tuck, body contouring) needs its own page targeting Fort Worth, Grapevine, and Southlake. This is how Dauwe and Obaid dominate local SEO.

2. **Build a "Why Duke Matters" section.** No other DFW surgeon can claim a full Duke plastic surgery residency. Create a compelling page explaining what Duke's program entails, why the integrated model produces better surgeons, and how it differs from the local UTSW pipeline. This is a permanent SEO asset for "Duke trained plastic surgeon Dallas."

3. **Develop a "Post-Weight-Loss Body Contouring" service page.** Target the GLP-1/Ozempic wave explicitly. Use keywords like "body contouring after Ozempic," "tummy tuck after weight loss," and "plastic surgery after GLP-1." Few DFW competitors are targeting this emerging segment with dedicated content.

4. **Publish a "Research & Publications" page.** List all 25+ publications with links. This builds E-E-A-T (Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, Trustworthiness) signals for Google and establishes academic credibility for research-driven patients.

5. **Add before-and-after galleries organized by procedure.** This is table stakes. Every competitor has them. Ensure they are high-quality, consistently photographed, and tagged with relevant alt text for SEO.

6. **Implement schema markup.** Add LocalBusiness, Physician, and MedicalBusiness structured data to every page. This helps Google surface Dr. Cason in local pack results and knowledge panels.

### Website -- Design and UX

7. **Benchmark against Dauwe's website.** dauweplasticsurgery.com is the design standard in DFW plastic surgery websites -- clean, modern, fast, mobile-optimized. Match or exceed this standard.

8. **Ensure the site loads in under 2 seconds on mobile.** Plastic surgery patients research on their phones. Site speed directly impacts Google rankings and conversion rates.

9. **Add a virtual consultation booking flow.** Reduce friction for Fort Worth patients who may perceive Grapevine as a drive.

### Content Marketing

10. **Launch a blog targeting Tier 5 keywords.** Publish 2-4 SEO-optimized articles per month covering patient education topics. Each article should target a specific long-tail keyword and link to the relevant procedure page.

11. **Tell the jazz-to-surgery story.** Create a compelling "About Dr. Cason" narrative that weaves the music background into the surgical philosophy. This is the kind of content that gets shared, linked to, and remembered. No other DFW surgeon has this story.

12. **Create comparison content (carefully).** Articles like "How to Choose a Plastic Surgeon in Fort Worth" or "What to Look for in a Board-Certified Plastic Surgeon" allow Dr. Cason to frame the selection criteria in terms of his own strengths (training pedigree, publications, dual expertise) without naming competitors.

### Social Media

13. **Accelerate Instagram growth from 15K toward 50K within 12 months.** Content strategy should include:
    - Before/after transformations (highest engagement in plastic surgery)
    - Educational reels explaining procedures (builds trust)
    - "Day in the life" content (humanizes the surgeon)
    - Patient testimonial videos (social proof)
    - Behind-the-scenes OR content (demonstrates expertise)

14. **Consider launching a podcast or video series.** Jejurikar's "Three Plastic Surgeons and a Microphone" has been a major brand-builder. Dr. Cason's music background gives him natural on-camera/on-mic charisma. A series like "The Surgeon's Eye" or similar could differentiate.

15. **Leverage TikTok for younger demographics.** Educational content about procedures, myth-busting, and recovery timelines performs well on TikTok for plastic surgeons targeting the 25-40 demographic.

### Reputation and Reviews

16. **Implement a systematic review generation process.** After every successful procedure, trigger a review request flow (email + text) directing patients to Google Business Profile first, then RealSelf. Target 50+ Google reviews within the first 12 months.

17. **Claim and optimize all directory listings.** Ensure consistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone) across Google Business Profile, Yelp, RealSelf, Healthgrades, WebMD, Vitals, US News, and Doximity. Inconsistencies hurt local SEO.

18. **Apply for D Magazine "Best Doctors in Dallas."** Multiple competitors (Jejurikar, Friedman, Adams) leverage this designation. It is a credibility marker that DFW patients recognize and trust.

### Strategic Moves

19. **Own the Fort Worth market.** No marquee plastic surgeon currently dominates Fort Worth proper. While Dr. Cason practices in Grapevine, aggressively targeting Fort Worth in SEO, Google Ads, and content positions him as the premier surgeon for the 900,000+ residents of Fort Worth and surrounding Tarrant County.

20. **Build the "post-weight-loss" niche.** The GLP-1 wave is creating a new patient population that needs body contouring, skin removal, breast lifts, and facial rejuvenation after significant weight loss. Dr. Cason's reconstructive training makes him uniquely qualified for these complex cases. First-mover advantage in this niche is available now.

21. **Develop referral relationships with GLP-1 prescribers.** Partner with weight-loss clinics, endocrinologists, and primary care physicians who prescribe Ozempic/Wegovy/Mounjaro. These providers have patients who will need plastic surgery in 12-18 months.

22. **Pursue PR and media placements.** The jazz-musician-to-Duke-surgeon story is inherently newsworthy. Pitch local media (D Magazine, Dallas Morning News, Fort Worth Star-Telegram, Southlake Style) and national plastic surgery media (The Plastic Surgery Channel, where Dr. Cason has already appeared on "The Digital Handshake").
