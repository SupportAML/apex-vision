# Sample Tweets — Dr. Roger Cason

## Educational (60%)

1. The #1 question I get about rhinoplasty: "Will it look natural?" The answer should always be yes. A great nose job doesn't announce itself — it just makes everything else look more harmonious. #Rhinoplasty #DallasPlasticSurgeon

2. Recovery myth: "I'll be back to normal in a week." Reality: You'll look presentable in 10-14 days, but final results from a facelift can take 3-6 months to fully settle. Patience is part of the process. #Facelift #PlasticSurgeryEducation

3. Thinking about a tummy tuck? Here's what most surgeons won't tell you upfront: the scar is real, it's permanent, and it matters WHERE it's placed. I spend significant time planning incision placement so it hides in your natural bikini line. #TummyTuck

4. "Board certified" and "board eligible" are not the same thing. Board certified means the surgeon passed rigorous exams AFTER completing residency. Always check. Your safety depends on it. #BoardCertifiedPlasticSurgeon

5. Botox isn't surgery. Fillers aren't surgery. But when they're done wrong, you may need surgery to fix them. Choose your injector as carefully as you'd choose your surgeon. #Aesthetics #DallasBeauty

6. Three things I look at in every rhinoplasty consultation: 1) Skin thickness 2) Nasal airway function 3) What bothers YOU most. The best results come from listening first, operating second. #NoseJob #Rhinoplasty

7. A mommy makeover isn't one procedure — it's a customized combination. Typically a tummy tuck + breast surgery, but every plan is different because every body is different. #MommyMakeover #DFWPlasticSurgery

8. Your plastic surgeon should be able to explain their training in one sentence. Mine: 6-year residency at Duke in plastic and reconstructive surgery. If they can't tell you clearly, keep looking.

9. Blepharoplasty (eyelid surgery) is one of the most underrated procedures in facial rejuvenation. Removing a small amount of excess skin can take 10 years off your appearance with minimal downtime. #Blepharoplasty

10. "How do I know if I need a facelift vs. just fillers?" If you're pulling your skin back in the mirror to see what you'd look like — that's what a facelift does. Fillers add volume. They solve different problems.

11. Breast augmentation isn't just about size. Shape, projection, implant placement (above vs. below the muscle), and incision location all matter. The consult is where the real work happens.

12. One thing I learned in 6 years at Duke: the best reconstructive surgeons make the best aesthetic surgeons. Rebuilding a face after trauma teaches you anatomy at a level that elevates everything else.

13. Sun damage is the #1 thing that ages your face — ahead of genetics, stress, and diet. Sunscreen isn't vanity. It's the most cost-effective anti-aging treatment that exists.

14. Revision rhinoplasty is harder than primary rhinoplasty. Scar tissue, altered anatomy, and a patient who's already been through it once. I take these cases seriously because the margin for error is smaller.

15. Thread lifts are marketed as "non-surgical facelifts." They're not. The results are temporary, the risks are real, and most patients I see who had them wish they'd just done the facelift. Ask the hard questions.

16. The best time to consider a tummy tuck is after you're done having children and at a stable weight. Operating too early means you may need a revision later. Timing matters.

17. What makes a rhinoplasty result "good"? You shouldn't be able to tell someone had surgery. The nose should fit the face — not look like it was ordered from a catalog.

18. Recovery from plastic surgery is not passive. How you care for yourself — nutrition, rest, following post-op instructions — directly impacts your results. Your surgeon can only do half the work.
