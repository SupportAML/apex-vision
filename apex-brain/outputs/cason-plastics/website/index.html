Meet Dr. Cason

Dr. Roger Cason is a board-certified plastic surgeon whose journey to aesthetic surgery began with one of the most rigorous training programs in the country: a six-year residency in plastic and reconstructive surgery at Duke University Medical Center.

At Duke, he mastered the full spectrum of plastic surgery: complex microsurgical reconstruction, craniofacial surgery, hand surgery, and every dimension of aesthetic surgery. That foundation in reconstruction is what makes his aesthetic work exceptional. He doesn't just know what looks good. He understands the architecture underneath.

With 25+ published papers and textbook chapters, Dr. Cason isn't just practicing plastic surgery. He's advancing it. His patients benefit from a surgeon who stays at the cutting edge of technique and evidence.

Common Questions

Answers to what patients ask most. For anything else, your consultation is the place.

Am I a good candidate for plastic surgery?

+
Good candidates are generally in good health, at a stable weight, have realistic expectations, and are motivated by personal goals rather than external pressure. The best way to find out is a consultation where Dr. Cason can evaluate your anatomy and discuss what's achievable.

What happens during a consultation?

+
You'll meet one-on-one with Dr. Cason to discuss your goals, medical history, and concerns. He'll perform an examination, explain your options, discuss realistic outcomes, and answer every question. Consultations are thorough and unhurried. There is never pressure to book.

Is Dr. Cason board certified?

+
Yes. Dr. Cason is certified by the American Board of Plastic Surgery, the only board recognized by the American Board of Medical Specialties for plastic surgery. This requires completing an accredited residency, passing rigorous exams, and maintaining ongoing education.

Will my results look natural?

+
Natural results are the standard, not the exception. Dr. Cason's philosophy centers on enhancing your existing features and restoring proportion rather than creating a "done" look. The best compliment his patients receive is "you look great" without anyone guessing why.

How long is recovery?

+
Recovery varies by procedure. Most patients return to desk work within 1-2 weeks and resume exercise by 4-6 weeks. Final results typically emerge over 3-6 months. Dr. Cason provides detailed recovery timelines during your consultation.

What financing options are available?

+
We offer financing through CareCredit and Prosper Healthcare Lending, with plans that fit most budgets. Our team can walk you through options during or after your consultation.

What if I need a revision?

+
Revision surgery is a core part of Dr. Cason's expertise. His reconstructive training at Duke gives him the technical skill to manage complex anatomy and scar tissue. He regularly takes on revision cases that other surgeons won't.

How do I prepare for surgery?

+
You'll receive detailed pre-operative instructions tailored to your procedure. Generally: stop smoking 4-6 weeks before, avoid blood-thinning medications, arrange for someone to drive you home, and prepare your recovery space.
