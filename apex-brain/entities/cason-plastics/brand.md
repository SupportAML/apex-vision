# Cason Plastic Surgery — Brand Guidelines

## Voice & Tone
- **Warm, confident, knowledgeable** — like a trusted friend who happens to be a world-class surgeon
- Speak plainly, not clinically — "tummy tuck" not "abdominoplasty" in patient-facing content
- Educate without lecturing — share knowledge generously, never condescendingly
- Confident but never arrogant — let credentials speak through the work, not boasting

## Personality Pillars
1. **Precision** — meticulous, detail-oriented, artistry in every procedure
2. **Warmth** — genuinely cares about patient outcomes and comfort
3. **Authority** — Duke-trained, published researcher, board-certified

## Visual Identity
- **Color palette:**
  - Primary: Warm charcoal (#2C2C2C) + Cream (#FAF7F2)
  - Accent: Soft gold (#C9A96E)
  - Supporting: Warm gray (#8A8A8A), White (#FFFFFF)
- **Typography:**
  - Headings: Playfair Display (serif — elegant, premium)
  - Body: Inter (sans-serif — clean, modern, highly readable)
- **Photography style:** Warm lighting, natural tones, professional but not sterile
- **Logo direction:** Clean wordmark, no clip-art medical symbols

## Content Do's
- Educate patients on what to expect (recovery, timelines, realistic outcomes)
- Humanize — share behind-the-scenes, OR prep, team moments
- Cite credentials naturally ("During my training at Duke...")
- Use before/after photos WITH context (timeline, procedure details)
- Engage with comments and questions authentically

## Content Don'ts
- No fear-based marketing ("Fix your flaws", "Don't let aging win")
- No price-focused messaging (avoid "$X starting at..." in social content)
- No before/after photos without written patient consent
- No exaggerated claims or "miracle" language
- No disparaging other surgeons or practices
- No medical advice that could be construed as a patient-doctor relationship
